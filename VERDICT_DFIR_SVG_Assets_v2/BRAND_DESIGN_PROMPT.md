# VERDICT DFIR — Master Rebrand Prompt

Rebrand VERDICT DFIR as an internet-native, Gen Z editorial evidence brand for digital forensics, incident response, and reproducible investigation.

## Positioning
- **Campaign:** Show Me the Evidence
- **Core thesis:** Don’t trust the model. Reproduce the finding.
- **Brand promise:** Evidence over assumption.
- **Tone:** confident, transparent, provocative, useful, and technically honest.

## Visual direction
- Think **digital research lab × independent magazine × annotated case file**.
- Use clean editorial grids, strong hierarchy, proof-path diagrams, real product crops, stamps, underlines, paper notes, and restrained tactile texture.
- Prioritize 3–6 word hooks and one visible proof artifact.
- Avoid esports, energy-drink, gaming, crypto, cyberpunk, rainbow gradients, neon overload, hooded hackers, fake terminals, and generic AI imagery.

## Palette
- Midnight Ink `#101426` — dark surfaces
- Electric Cobalt `#4D5DFF` — primary brand
- Soft Lilac `#B8A8FF` — secondary brand
- Paper Cream `#F5F1E8` — primary light surface
- Seafoam `#73D9C2` — verified / replay matched
- Signal Coral `#FF6257` — rejected / contradiction / failure
- Butter Yellow `#FFD76A` — annotation / review
- Near Black `#12131A` — light-mode text

Use one semantic accent per composition. Do not use coral, seafoam, and yellow decoratively.

## Logo
Use the clean evidence-path V: two evidence branches converge into one decision node. The left branch is cobalt; the right branch is lilac. No glitch, brush, lightning, shield, lock, or rainbow treatment. Preserve generous clear space.

## Typography
- Logo and body: a clean modern sans similar to Inter.
- Editorial headlines: a heavy condensed sans.
- Evidence, hashes, timestamps, and paths: a technical monospace.
- Human notes: a restrained handwritten face used only for annotations.

## Thumbnail psychology
1. One conflict or unresolved question.
2. Main hook limited to 3–6 words.
3. One real proof artifact or product crop.
4. Clear semantic state: VERIFIED, REJECTED, FOUND, MISSED, or REPLAY FAILED.
5. Honest curiosity—never imply a result the content does not prove.

## Required applications
Website, product dashboard, GitHub README, docs, YouTube, TikTok, LinkedIn, X, Reddit, decks, stickers, reports, and merchandise.
